Food and housing

SNAP (food stamps)
Income up to 130% FPL (~$1,580/month single) ‚Äî many states have higher limits
Average benefit: $188/month for individuals. Apply at your state's SNAP office or benefits.gov. Students and people who are employed can qualify.
Section 8 / Housing Choice Voucher
Income under 50% of area median income
Pays the difference between 30% of your income and actual rent. Waitlists are long but applications are free. Apply at your local Public Housing Authority when lists open.
LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance)
Income under 150% FPL
Pays heating and cooling bills. Often covers past-due balances to prevent shutoffs. Apply through your state energy office or at benefits.gov.

Internet access

Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) successor programs
Income at or below 200% FPL, or enrolled in qualifying programs
The ACP ended in 2024 but individual ISPs have replacement programs. Check with Comcast (Internet Essentials), AT&T (Access), and your state's digital equity programs. Many offer $10‚Äì$30/month internet.

Education

Pell Grant
Undergrad students with demonstrated financial need
Up to $7,395/year for college. Does not have to be repaid. Fill out the FAFSA at studentaid.gov ‚Äî it's also the gateway to all other federal aid.

Phones

Lifeline Program
Income at or below 135% FPL, or enrolled in Medicaid/SNAP/SSI
$9.25/month discount on phone or broadband. Some providers (Safelink, Q Link) offer free phones + service. Apply at lifelinesupport.org
