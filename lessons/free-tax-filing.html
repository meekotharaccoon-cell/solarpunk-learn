Free Tax Filing — Every Legitimate Option

TurboTax and H&R Block spend millions lobbying to keep taxes complicated. Filing is free if you know where to look. Most people qualify for fully free filing.

⚠️ First: avoid these

TurboTax Free Edition and H&R Block Free Online are designed to upsell you. The free versions are limited and they aggressively push upgrades. Use the options below instead — they're actually free.

Your options, best first

IRS Free File — irs.gov/freefile
Under $79,000 AGI · free federal + most state returns
The IRS partners with tax software companies to offer truly free filing if you earn under $79,000. Go through the IRS website directly — going to the software companies' websites directly may not give you the free version. Use the IRS's "Find Your Trusted Partner" tool.
IRS Direct File — directfile.irs.gov
Available in 24+ states · simple returns · 100% free
The IRS's own filing tool. No third party. No upsells. Works for W-2 income, standard deduction, limited credits. If your taxes are simple, this is the cleanest option.
VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance)
Under $67,000 AGI · in-person · free professional prep
IRS-trained volunteers prepare your return for free. Usually at libraries, community centers, churches. Find a site: irs.gov/vita or call 211. This is professional quality, not amateur help.
AARP Tax-Aide
Any age · any income · free · in-person and virtual
Despite the name, open to all ages. IRS-certified preparers. Good for older filers, disability income, Social Security questions. Find locations: aarpfoundation.org/taxaide
FreeTaxUSA — freetaxusa.com
Any income · federal always free · state $15
Legitimately free federal filing at any income level. State returns cost $15 (much less than competitors). Handles itemized deductions, self-employment, investments. No upsell pressure.

Credits people miss

Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): Up to $7,830 for families. Many eligible people don't claim it. Check eligibility at irs.gov/eitc

Child and Dependent Care Credit: Up to 35% of care expenses if you pay for childcare to work.

Premium Tax Credit: If you bought insurance through the marketplace, you may be owed money back even if you chose not to receive it upfront.

Student Loan Interest: Deduct up to $2,500 in student loan interest even if you don't itemize.

Home Office Deduction: If you're self-employed and work from home, a portion of rent/mortgage, utilities, and internet is deductible.

✅ File even if you owe nothing

Even with zero income you may be owed refundable credits. And a filed return starts the statute of limitations on IRS audits. Not filing has no upside.
