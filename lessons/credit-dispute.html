One in five credit reports contains an error. Errors can cost you thousands in higher interest rates. Disputing them is free, federally mandated, and takes under an hour.

A 50-point credit score difference can mean $100‚Äì$300 more per month on a mortgage. One corrected error can save you tens of thousands over a loan's lifetime.

Step 1: Get your free reports

You're entitled to one free report from each bureau (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) every 12 months ‚Äî federally mandated under the FCRA.

The only legitimate free site: annualcreditreport.com ‚Äî everything else is a subscription trap.

Pull all three. They don't share data with each other, so errors often appear on one and not the others.

Step 2: Read them like a detective

Check every line. Common errors:

  • Accounts that aren't yours (identity mix-up or fraud)
  • Late payments that were actually on time
  • Closed accounts still showing as open
  • Debts past the 7-year reporting limit still listed
  • Incorrect balances or credit limits
  • Duplicate accounts listed twice

Step 3: File the dispute

Dispute directly with the bureau reporting the error ‚Äî not the creditor.

  • Equifax: equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services/credit-dispute
  • Experian: experian.com/disputes
  • TransUnion: transunion.com/credit-disputes

Online disputes waive some rights. Send a certified letter with return receipt. This creates a paper trail and triggers the 30-day legal deadline.

What to include in the letter

  1. Your full name, address, date of birth, SSN last 4
  2. Exactly which item you're disputing (account name, number)
  3. Why it's wrong (brief, factual)
  4. What you want: removed, corrected, updated
  5. Copies (not originals) of any supporting documents

What happens next

Under the FCRA, the bureau must investigate within 30 days and respond in writing. If they can't verify the item, they must delete it.

If the dispute is ignored or improperly handled, you can sue under the FCRA. Statutory damages: $100‚Äì$1,000 per violation. Plus actual damages and attorney fees (attorneys often take these on contingency ‚Äî no cost to you).

Request a free updated copy of your report. If the error was corrected, you can ask the bureau to notify anyone who pulled your report in the last 6 months.

If the dispute fails

File a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau at consumerfinance.gov/complaint. This creates a federal record and often moves companies faster than a dispute alone.
