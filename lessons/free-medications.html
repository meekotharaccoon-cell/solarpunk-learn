How to Get Free or Almost-Free Medications

Pharmaceutical companies are legally required to offer free medications to people who can't afford them. Most people don't know this. The programs exist. Here's how to access them.

Every major pharmaceutical company has a Patient Assistance Program (PAP). They give away billions in medications every year. The income threshold is often up to $40,000‚Äì$60,000 for a single person. No insurance required in many programs.

Google "[drug name] patient assistance program." Every major drug has one. Call the company directly. Say: "I'm uninsured and can't afford this medication. Do you have a patient assistance program?" They must tell you.

Every major insulin manufacturer offers free insulin programs:

  • Novo Nordisk: Patient Assistance Program ‚Äî free for qualifying patients
  • Sanofi: Insulins Valyou Savings Program

Also: Walmart sells ReliOn brand insulin over the counter for $25/vial. Not ideal for everyone but has kept people alive.
